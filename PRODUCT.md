# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, senior engineers, and technical leads evaluating Steven as a candidate or collaborator. They arrive via a resume link, a GitHub profile, or a cold referral. They're looking for signal quickly: can this person build things? Are they thoughtful? Would working with them be good? The site has thirty seconds to earn five minutes.

Secondary: fellow engineers who found a blog post or repo and are curious about the person behind it.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Shiqi Hu ("Steven"), a full stack engineer. The site exists to turn a résumé line into a person — to make a hiring manager feel like they've already met him before the first call. Success means a recruiter shares the link with an engineering lead because it stood out.

The site lives at shiqihu.com. It covers engineering work (experience, projects, skills), a photography interest section that reveals character, and a contact form. No external auth. No CMS. Content is code.

## Brand Personality

Lighthearted · Quirky · Ambitious

- **Lighthearted**: doesn't take itself too seriously; the copy has a pulse. Warmth without trying.
- **Quirky**: small unexpected moments — a wry line, an unusual detail, a choice that makes you look twice. Not weird for weird's sake; personality for the right reasons.
- **Ambitious**: the work speaks to scale and craft; the site doesn't undersell itself. Not boastful — confident. The kind of engineer who builds things that last.

Voice in writing: like a senior engineer who's good enough to be relaxed about it. First person, specific, occasionally funny.

## Reference

Apple's design: extreme negative space as a design element, typography as primary visual statement, confident precision in spacing and alignment, motion that reveals rather than decorates, one dominant idea per fold. The reference is the craft and restraint, not any specific color or component pattern.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev portfolio**: dark-mode hero with "Hi, I'm a developer" in giant text, skills progress bars, identical project cards in a 3-column grid. Invisible.
- **Corporate LinkedIn résumé**: blue-and-white, professional-headshot energy, every section reads like a job description, quantified achievements stacked in bullets. Forgettable.
- **Brutalist / maximalist**: stark black-white grid, deliberately rough, heavy unconventional type. Wrong tone — this person builds polished things; the site should feel like the product.
- **Over-polished agency site**: cursor effects, scroll-jacking, parallax for its own sake, motion that distracts from content. Style over substance.

## Design Principles

1. **Content earns its space** — generous whitespace is a deliberate choice, not an absence of ideas. Each element is there because it has something to say; nothing is filler.
2. **Personality without performance** — the quirk lives in copy and small well-chosen moments, not in dramatic scroll effects. The site earns attention before asking for it.
3. **Ambition, quietly stated** — the work demonstrates scale and craft. The site doesn't proclaim it; it shows it. Show, don't subtitle.
4. **Precision at every scale** — alignment, spacing, and typographic rhythm matter at the detail level. Photography is here because Steven has an eye for composition; the site should too.
5. **Imagery is character** — the photography section is not decorative. It is the difference between a résumé and a person. It belongs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum throughout; AA for all body text and interactive elements.
- Keyboard navigable: all interactive elements (nav, lightbox, form) reachable and operable without a mouse.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected globally: all entrance and scroll animations reduce to instant transitions or crossfades.
- `prefers-color-scheme` respected on first load; stored preference applied on return visits.
- Alt text written to voice standard, not accessibility checkbox. Descriptive and specific.
